System Advisor Model (SAM)

System Advisor Model (SAM) modules are used to perform energy calculations. The program uses the SAM SDK API (SSC Version: 159; Mar 14 2016)

The System Advisor Model (“Model”) is provided by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (“NREL”), which is operated by the Alliance for Sustainable Energy, LLC (“Alliance”) for the U.S. Department Of Energy (“DOE”) and may be used for any purpose whatsoever.

SAM uses meteorological and solar data to model output of various renewable energy technologies. MERRA-2 data is a suitable source to generate data files for input to SAM.

MERRA-2 data

The Modern Era Retrospective-analysis for Research and Applications, Version 2 (MERRA-2) is a NASA atmospheric data reanalysis for the satellite era using a major new version of the Goddard Earth Observing System Data Assimilation System Version 5 (GEOS-5). MERRA-2 focuses on historical analyses of the hydrological cycle on a broad range of weather and climate time scales, and places the NASA EOS suite of observations in a climate context. This data is made available through the Global Modeling and Assimilation Office (GMAO) and the GES DISC.

SWIS Load and Other Data

SIREN uses current load data to model system performance against actual load. The data is obtained from the Australian Energy Market Operator (AEMO; formerly from the Independent Market Operator, IMO). The AEMO collects and collates a range of data relating to the Wholesale Electricity Market (WEM) within the South West Interconnected System (SWIS) in Western Australia.
